### Tests

- `crates/cad-core/tests/round_fillet_brep_chain_resolution_smoke.rs` —
  new integration test file (cargo-discovered, no manifest change). Two
  tests, both proving a committed `CuboidOp -> RoundFilletOp` graph
  chain preserves the upstream Cuboid's B-Rep face and edge identity
  vectors through the public graph resolvers:
  - `radius_variation_preserves_cuboid_face_and_edge_ids`: builds the
    chain with three valid positive radii (`0.05`, `0.1`, `0.25`) on the
    same single-edge selection (`ref_edges[0]`). Asserts the resolved
    RoundFillet-node face IDs (`brep_face_ids_for_node`) and edge IDs
    (`brep_edge_ids_for_node`) are `assert_eq!`-equal to the direct
    upstream Cuboid vectors for every radius. Also pins 6 faces / 12
    edges.
  - `selected_edge_variation_preserves_cuboid_face_and_edge_ids`: builds
    the chain at a fixed radius (`0.1`) for three selections — a
    single-edge selection, a multi-edge selection
    (`[ref_edges[0], ref_edges[3], ref_edges[7]]`), and all 12 Cuboid
    edges. Asserts resolved face/edge vectors `assert_eq!`-equal the
    direct upstream Cuboid vectors, and that every filleted edge
    survives in the resolved edge vector.
  - Helpers: `owner()` (fixed `BRepOwnerId`), `fixture_cuboid()`
    (deterministic non-cubic `CuboidOp` 2.0 × 1.5 × 1.0),
    `upstream_refs()` (direct `cuboid.brep_face_ids(owner)` mapped to
    `Vec<BRepFaceId>` in emission order + `cuboid.brep_edge_ids(owner)`
    as `Vec<BRepEdgeId>`), and `resolve_round_fillet_chain()` (builds /
    commits one `Cuboid -> RoundFillet` graph and resolves at the
    RoundFillet node).
  - Uses only public `rge_cad_core` APIs (`CadGraph`, `OperatorNode`,
    `BRepOwnerId`, `CuboidOp`, `RoundFilletOp`, `BRepProvider`,
    `BRepEdgeProvider`, `BRepFaceId`, `BRepEdgeId`,
    `brep_face_ids_for_node`, `brep_edge_ids_for_node`). No private
    cad-core modules and no RoundFillet internals (`RoundFilletSpec`,
    `RoundFilletPathSpec`, `RoundFilletUpstream`) are imported. Resolver
    vectors are compared with `assert_eq!` and are not sorted,
    deduplicated, filtered, or normalized.
