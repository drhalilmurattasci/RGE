### Tests

- `crates/material-graph/src/lib.rs::tests::snapshot_ron_round_trip_preserves_material_payloads`:
  builds a populated `MaterialGraph` with three material nodes
  (`albedo`, `normal`, `output`) and two typed-port edges
  (`albedo->output` as `Color->Color`, `normal->output` as
  `Vector->Texture`). It captures the private `g.graph` field with
  `GraphSnapshot::from_graph`, serializes via `GraphSnapshot::to_ron`,
  deserializes via `GraphSnapshot::from_ron`, restores via
  `GraphSnapshot::to_graph`, then asserts the restored substrate `Graph`
  preserves:
  - node count and edge count;
  - node identity ‚Äî the full `Vec<(NodeId, MaterialNode)>` of the original
    equals that of the restored graph (pins each `NodeId` and stored
    material node key);
  - edge identity ‚Äî the full `Vec<(EdgeId, EdgeRecord<MaterialEdge>)>` of
    the original equals that of the restored graph (pins each `EdgeId`,
    `src`, `dst`, and `MaterialEdge` port payload);
  - plus concrete spot-checks of both edges addressed by the `EdgeId`
    returned at construction time, comparing real restored `src`/`dst`/
    `MaterialEdge` payloads against the values built in the test.
  The test imports `EdgeRecord` and `GraphSnapshot` from
  `rge_kernel_graph_foundation`; it uses the in-crate test module's access
  to the private `MaterialGraph.graph` field and private `MaterialNode`
  type, so no public snapshot/inner-graph accessor was introduced.
