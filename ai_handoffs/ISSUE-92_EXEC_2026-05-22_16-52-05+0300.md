## Five-Question Preflight Answer Block

1. **What cache surfaces exist today in `io-gltf` and `io-image`, and how do
   they differ from `rge-asset-store::Cache` plus `LocalCache`?**

   Two locally-owned stubs, structurally **not the same shape** as
   `rge-asset-store::Cache`:

   - `io-gltf::cache_stub::Cache` (`crates/io-gltf/src/cache_stub.rs:28`)
     is a **typed asset-family cache** with five `insert_*`/`get_*` pairs
     keyed by per-family content-hash handles (`MeshHandle` / `MaterialHandle`
     / `AnimationHandle` / `SkeletonHandle` / `ImageHandle`). Get returns a
     **borrowed** `Option<&T>` of the decoded asset; insert is infallible
     and `&mut self`. Backed by an in-process `HashMap`-of-typed-assets
     `MemoryCache` (`cache_stub.rs:63`).
   - `io-image::asset_store_stub::Cache`
     (`crates/io-image/src/asset_store_stub.rs:23`) is a **byte-blob
     cache** with `put(&self, Vec<u8>) -> AssetId`, `get(&self, &AssetId)
     -> Option<Vec<u8>>`, `has(...)`. Keys are an opaque `Vec<u8>`
     (`asset_store_stub.rs:20`), **not content-addressed** — the stub
     `MemoryCache` derives ids from a counter + blob length
     (`asset_store_stub.rs:49-58`), so two identical blobs collide on
     content but not on id (the opposite of the asset-store contract).
     The module is `pub mod` but unconsumed in-crate.

   `rge-asset-store::Cache` (`crates/asset-store/src/cache.rs:41`) is a
   **byte-blob cache** with `Result`-wrapped fallible `get`/`put`,
   `evict_lru`, `total_size`, `len`, `is_empty`. Keys are
   `rge_kernel_asset::AssetId` (BLAKE3 32-byte digest, content-addressed,
   string-form `blake3:<hex>`). `put` is idempotent: same bytes → same
   id, second call no-op storage-wise. `LocalCache`
   (`crates/asset-store/src/local.rs:57`) is the production
   filesystem-backed impl with deterministic monotonic-tick LRU,
   atomic temp+rename writes, `.index` recency file, reopen-survives
   reconciliation.

   Net: `io-gltf`'s stub is **typed and borrowing**; `asset-store` is
   **untyped byte-blob and owning-on-get with `Result`**. The
   `io-image` stub is closer in shape to `asset-store` (single byte
   lane) but loses content-addressed determinism and has no consumers
   forcing the question yet.

2. **Which `io-*` cache surfaces should route through `rge-asset-store`,
   and which should remain local or test-only?**

   - **`io-image::asset_store_stub`** is the candidate to be deleted in
     favour of `rge-asset-store::Cache`. It has zero in-tree consumers
     beyond its own unit tests (`crates/io-image/src/asset_store_stub.rs:76-93`),
     its stub `MemoryCache` is non-content-addressed and explicitly
     placeholder, and its trait shape (byte-blob `get`/`put`/`has`) is
     a near-subset of `asset-store::Cache`. The doc-string already
     names the migration (`asset_store_stub.rs:1-11`). Replacing it
     does not require touching any io-image codec or test fixture.
   - **`io-gltf::cache_stub::Cache` / `MemoryCache`** should **not** be
     swapped wholesale to `asset-store::Cache` directly. The two traits
     are structurally incompatible: io-gltf's surface is typed
     per-family with **borrowed-return** get (`Option<&MeshAsset>`,
     `cache_stub.rs:32`), while asset-store's is byte-blob with
     **owned-return-and-fallible** get (`Result<Option<Bytes>,
     CacheError>`, `cache.rs:51`). A real integration would have to
     either (a) introduce a `TypedCache` adapter that serialises each
     asset family to canonical bytes for `asset-store::Cache::put` and
     deserialises plus caches on `get`, or (b) keep `cache_stub` as a
     thin owning typed-decoded layer above an asset-store byte-blob
     backing store. Either is a non-trivial design call that belongs
     to a future dispatch, not the smallest-next-step.
   - The remainder (per-codec parsing, mip generation, importer scene
     building) is stateless format adaptation and should stay local —
     it owns no cache state to route.

3. **Does `kernel/asset-view` become a genuine consumer once `io-gltf`
   binds to `rge-asset-store`, or does it remain a future descriptor
   substrate?**

   **It remains a future descriptor substrate.** `kernel/asset-view`
   today is a passive vocabulary substrate: `AssetViewRegistry` is a
   `BTreeMap`-backed lookup of `ViewDescriptor { id, kind, byte_len }`
   (`kernel/asset-view/src/registry.rs:41-46`), `ViewKind` has a single
   `Placeholder` variant (`descriptor.rs:21-24`), and the crate's own
   NON-GOALS list explicitly excludes buffer ownership, WASM
   linear-memory mapping, `kernel/asset` integration, residency, GPU
   upload, and IO scheduling (`lib.rs:13-39`). It has **zero
   non-self consumers**: `rg` for `AssetViewRegistry` /
   `AssetViewId` / `ViewDescriptor` outside the crate finds only
   `wgpu::TextureViewDescriptor` matches in `crates/gfx/**` and
   `crates/editor-shell/**` plus one documentation cross-reference in
   `crates/cad-core/src/topology/face_id/mod.rs:56`. Its `Cargo.toml`
   lists only `serde`/`serde_json` — no `rge-asset-store`, no
   `rge-io-gltf`, no `rge-kernel-asset`.

   Binding `io-image` (or even `io-gltf`) to `rge-asset-store::Cache`
   does **not** add pressure on `asset-view`. The cache trait yields
   owned `Bytes` (`crates/asset-store/src/cache.rs:51`), it does not
   produce read-only borrowed slices, and the importer / exporter
   currently has no descriptor-vocabulary consumer waiting for it.
   Genuine consumer pressure would arrive when (a) a real `ViewKind`
   variant lands (e.g. `MeshVertices` / `Texture2D`) backed by a
   `kernel/asset-streaming` residency owner that produces the read-only
   slice, or (b) a WASM-bridge call site needs to surface a registered
   descriptor to a script. Neither exists today.

4. **What is the smallest next dispatch that swaps one `io-*` cache to
   the asset-store `Cache` trait without churning the kernel cavity?**
